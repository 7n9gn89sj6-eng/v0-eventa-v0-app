# Linking Rules and Conventions

## Core Principle

The main text must always read cleanly on its own. Links are optional side doors that provide additional understanding, not required footnotes.

## When to Link

### Link to Lightbox (2–4 sentences)

Link when:
- A term appears for the first time and may be unfamiliar
- The term is central to understanding the passage
- A brief explanation would enhance clarity without disrupting flow

Do not link when:
- The term is used repeatedly and context makes its meaning clear
- The link would interrupt the rhythm of the text
- The term is peripheral to the main point

### Link to Notes (300–600 words)

Link when:
- A deeper explanation would enrich understanding
- Historical or cultural context is relevant but too detailed for the main text
- The topic warrants extended exploration

Use sparingly. Most content should not require notes links.

## How to Link

### In Main Text

- Use natural, integrated links that feel part of the sentence
- Avoid phrases like "click here" or "see more"
- Let the linked word or phrase carry the meaning

Example (good):
"The journeys extend across the Hellenic world, from the Gates of Hercules to the Black Sea."

Example (avoid):
"For more information about the Gates of Hercules, click here."

### Lightbox Links

- Trigger a lightbox popup with a brief explanation
- Should feel like a natural pause, not an interruption
- Close easily and return to the main text

### Notes Links

- Open a separate page with deeper context
- Clearly marked as "Notes" or similar
- Should feel like an invitation, not an obligation

## Language-Specific Rules

### Hellas

- Link on first mention only (with "Greece" in parentheses)
- Thereafter, use "Hellas" without linking
- Exception: If the page is specifically about the term itself

### Hellenes

- Link on first mention if the distinction from "Greeks" is important
- Otherwise, use without linking if context is clear

### Hellenic World

- Link on first mention if the concept is central
- May link again if the term appears after a long gap or in a new context

### Geographic Terms

- Link "Gates of Hercules" and "Black Sea" when used in their cultural geography sense
- Do not link when used simply as geographic references

## Tone and Style

- Links should feel helpful, not pedantic
- Avoid over-linking; trust the reader
- When in doubt, do not link
- The text should stand alone; links are a bonus

## Technical Notes for Wix

- Lightboxes: Use Wix's lightbox feature for short explanations
- Notes pages: Create separate pages accessible via links
- Test that links feel natural and don't disrupt reading flow
