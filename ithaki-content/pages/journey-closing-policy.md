# Journey Closing Line Policy

## Baseline (default)

The journey is built around return, attention, and the understanding that comes from moving slowly through places shaped by long histories of movement and exchange.

## Acceptable Variants

### Variant 1 (geography-focused)
The journey follows older routes of connection, moving at a pace that allows the layers of settlement, exchange, and continuity to become visible.

### Variant 2 (time-focused)
The journey is built around return and attention — returning to places, paying attention to what persists, and understanding how past and present remain in dialogue.

### Variant 3 (movement-focused)
The journey moves slowly, on foot, allowing time for the patterns of movement and settlement that have shaped this place to become clear.

## Rule for Variation

**Default:** Use the baseline closing line for all journeys.

**Vary only when:**
- The geography clearly demands emphasis (e.g., a journey specifically about routes → Variant 1)
- The method of movement is central to the journey's character (e.g., a journey built entirely around walking → Variant 3)
- The theme of return or time is the primary focus (e.g., a journey that revisits the same places → Variant 2)

**Do not vary:**
- For novelty or to avoid repetition
- To match the journey's name or opening
- To add promotional emphasis

## Constraints

- One sentence only
- Reflective, not invitational
- No marketing language
- Aligned with: understanding over distance, attention over accumulation, continuity over novelty
- Reusable across journeys
